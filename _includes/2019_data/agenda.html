Program

Summary

Primarily aimed at doctoral candidates at EPFL, this summer school will give a general overview of what Open Science can mean in practice.

Four morning sessions will be dedicated to background information on Open Science (Day 1), publications (Day 2), data (Day 3), and code (Day 4). Each afternoon will be dedicated to hands-on activities, such as the identification of disciplinary standards (Day 1), channels for knowledge dissemination (Day 2), how to describe and reuse research data (Day 3), and best practice for sharing code and methods (Day 4). The last day (Day 5) of the week-long course will be open to everyone from EPFL and will focus on researchers who are already active on campus, or who are interested in Open Science. This will be followed by presentations from participants. In teams, they will summarize the topics they worked on during the workshop.
